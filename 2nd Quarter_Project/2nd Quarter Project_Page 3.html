I often cram for assessments; I've learned to navigate through various aspects of life by occasionally adopting the mañana habit. Balancing academics, social life, extracurricular activities, and even a love life can be challenging, but I've found ways to handle it effectively, even when facing tight deadlines. While there are days when I'm motivated and practice solid time management, I must admit that procrastination is a frequent companion. However, on the eve of the deadline, I turn to the Pomodoro technique to help manage my time efficiently. Breaking down my assessment work into focused 50-minute sessions with 10-minute breaks allows me to stay productive. It's a strategy that has worked well for me, and I recommend it to fellow students. To make life easier, my key advice is to establish a schedule and stick to it without any excuses. This disciplined approach can alleviate stress and ensure a smoother academic journey. For those who, like me, occasionally succumb to procrastination, incorporating Pomodoro intervals can be a game-changer. Adjust the time intervals to suit your preferences, and you might find yourself more focused and productive.

I get through and get ready for my classes every day as a student in PSHS-SRC batch ‘27 by first accomplishing my morning routine in the dorm. When I wake up; I immediately do some stretches, tidy my bed, brush my teeth, wash my face, shower, get changed, and prepare my things for school. After that, I head to the canteen to eat breakfast with my friends. What helps me get through my classes is my friendship with other people. It makes the challenging and pressuring school environment less taxing and more bearable. After classes, I hang out with them to regain my energy and have fun. At times, we also ask each other for help in certain subjects and exchange notes.
