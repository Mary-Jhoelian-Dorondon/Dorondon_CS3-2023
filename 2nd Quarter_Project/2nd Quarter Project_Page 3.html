I often cram for assessments; I've learned to navigate through various aspects of life by occasionally adopting the ma√±ana habit. Balancing academics, social life, extracurricular activities, and even a love life can be challenging, but I've found ways to handle it effectively, even when facing tight deadlines. While there are days when I'm motivated and practice solid time management, I must admit that procrastination is a frequent companion. However, on the eve of the deadline, I turn to the Pomodoro technique to help manage my time efficiently. Breaking down my assessment work into focused 50-minute sessions with 10-minute breaks allows me to stay productive. It's a strategy that has worked well for me, and I recommend it to fellow students. To make life easier, my key advice is to establish a schedule and stick to it without any excuses. This disciplined approach can alleviate stress and ensure a smoother academic journey. For those who, like me, occasionally succumb to procrastination, incorporating Pomodoro intervals can be a game-changer. Adjust the time intervals to suit your preferences, and you might find yourself more focused and productive.

In completing my assessments, I always manage my time. Usually, I start with the easiest task and move on to the hardest task. And I avoid doing them the night before the deadline. Moreover, I ask for help from friends for tasks that are difficult for me to complete on my own. By doing so, I am given more time to study for quizzes and work on personal goals that are not related to school. A tip I can share for students would be completing the assessments right after it was given or days before the designated deadline.
